Web Dashboard Design

UX/UI

ROLE

UI Design, User flows, Wireframing, Prototyping

DURATION

June - July 2019

TOOLS

Sketch, pen + paper, Invision, Invision Craft

SUMMARY

A client was looking for a re-design of their referrals dashboard in order to improve their user experience and visual design. This referrals dashboard needed to be kept simple and easy to understand. During the process of this redesign, the client changed their mind of how they envisioned the new dashboard based on a competitor that I introduced in my research.

PROBLEM

The website had a few UX issues and the UI design was not as great as it could be. There were also issues with the mobile design.

THE PROCESS

My approach to this redesign was in the following phases: Empathy, Discovery, Structure, UI and Usability Testing. I also had to keep in mind that this was a white-label product, so the requirements were that the design had to include a primary, secondary and two accent colors. However, the client was open to changing these requirements.

In the beginning

EMPATHY

I started my research on affiliate marketers, which was the client’s targeted audience. Once I felt like I had gathered enough information, I constructed a user persona and user journey map of the current referral dashboard flow. This allowed me to emphasize and understand who I was designing for, their needs and find pain points in the current flow.

Discovery

My next step was to research competitors and designs that are out in the world. The competitors that I looked at were ReferralCandy, FirstPromoter and Airbnb’s referral program.

ReferralCandy

ReferralCandy is a referral program service that is focused on users in e-commerce. It helps users send out referral emails to their customers in which they can obtain discounts or other offers for successful referrals

  • Prevents self-referrals
  • Users can see a preview of what their emails will look like for both mobile and web, before sending it out to customers. These emails come with themes which are customizable but the text cannot be edited
  • Shows a preview of what it will look like to share on Facebook and Twitter

FirstPromoter (affiliate) dashboard

FirstPromoter is a service which allows its clients to manage and track their own referral programs and affiliate marketers that work for the clients.

  • Doesn’t indicate what tab you’re on which affects the promoter’s understanding of where they are
  • Doesn’t show earnings on the dashboard, which is important if the goal for a promoter is to get rewards
  • Mobile-friendly but opening menu is jarring. It just pushes down the content

Airbnb

Airbnb has a referral program in which users can receive up to $38 CAD in credit for every person who completes a stay.

  • Is for regular users who would send promotions to their friends. Therefore there is no dashboard or analytics. Airbnb handles the contents of the email so those who send referrals to their friends cannot not see what it looks like
  • The tone is focused on inviting friends and sharing a common interest, which is travel
  • Tells the user clearly what they’re gaining and giving to their friends

This is where the client changed their vision for this redesign. They did not want to create a dashboard that was advanced like FirstPromoter or ReferralCandy, and wanted to go with a smaller approach like Airbnb.

RECOMMENDATIONS FOR THE CLIENT

After analyzing the competitors, I created a list of recomendations that could be added for the redesign and discussed it with the client. My recommendations were:

  • Include a call to action on the landing page
  • Allow users to import CSV files
  • Email theme customization
  • Add an invite link

After speaking to the client and presenting my findings, we narrowed down the changes that we agreed should be implemented into the new design

In the end

RECOMMENDATIONS

Moving on, I researched into features and solutions to how the client could improve their user retention rate as well as the overall experience and interest of the mobile app.

TO IMPROVE USER RETENTION

Functionality and first impressions: Ensure a high quality app

  • Splash screen image shifts
  • ‘Meds’ takes a while to load
  • Grammar issues
  • Android back button not working for a few screens

Use push notifications to remind the user of your app

  • Encourage and remind the user to use the app
  • Updates on blog posts
  • Ask the user to rate the app
  • Upgrade account to pro if they haven’t already

Include user personalization to create a more unique and relevant experience

  • Health profiles
  • Tracking symptoms
  • Allowing users to personalize the articles that they can see

OTHER RECOMMENDATIONS

  • Let users choose if they want to chat with a doctor right away once they register
  • Give users the ability to create a medical profile for themselves
  • Ability to let users stay in queue when they exit the popup
  • Let users access the chat history, but not open the chat logs if they are not a pro member

The last deliverable for the client was a mockup design that I created to show what the mobile app could look like in the future.

THE OUTCOMES

After the UX audit was presented, the client applied a few of the recommendations that I had made and saw that they had an increase in user retention from 30% to 50%.

THE STRUGGLES

It was challenging to conduct an analysis of the mobile app when I could not talk to their users. There were times when I was watching the UX Cam recordings and wished that I could ask the user why they did what they did. It was also a challenge for me to look into their Google analytics data since I had never done that before.
