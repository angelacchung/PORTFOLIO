Ecommerce Website

UX/UI

ROLE

UI Design, Wireframing, Userflows, Sitemapping, Prototyping

TOOLS

Sketch, Whimsical, Invision, Craft Invision

DURATION

4 weeks

SUMMARY

A client was looking to have their idea of an e-commerce store be brought to life and I was the UX/UI Designer who led this project from conception to execution. I made both mobile and desktop designs for this project.

THE PROCESS

1. Stakeholder Interviews

2. User Research

3. Structure

4. UI Design

5. Prototype

6. Usability Testing

THE CHALLENGE

The client wanted to integrate a 3-D house or 3-D rooms into the shopping experience. This 3-D feature would not be the primary shopping experience, but rather for the purpose for users to find inspiration/browse and to be set apart from competitors. This project was a huge challenge for me, as I’ve never designed a project at this scale alone.

In the beginning

PERSONAS

In order to understand who the targeted users were, I created 3 user personas based on the information that the client provided. These user personas were visual guides that helped me comprehend who I was designing for.

USER JOURNEY MAPS

The next step I took was taking these personas and creating user journey maps. These user journey maps were based off of typical ecommerce shopping experiences. I used these as visual maps in order to find and show the client where pain points are found. I also showed them possible opportunities that could be implemented in each stage of the user journey maps.

THE TONE

The aim for this ecommerce website was to have a tone that reflected a comfortable, welcoming and warm feel. I made sure to reflect this tone and also implemented moments that showed the organization that they were 'curators' and that they cared about the customer.

The structure

SITE MAPPING

Based off of the user flow, I created a site map of the ecommerce store. This helped me keep track of what screens will be in the site.

WIREFLOWS

Once I had the user flow approved, I made the wireframes which I then turned into wireflows. This was just to show to the client so they could have a visual map of how the ecommerce store would work.

UI DESIGNS

After some wireframe changes, the client provided their logo and colors. Based on these assets, I moved on to create the UI designs.

In the end

PROTOTYPE

A prototype of the 3D rooms was created, since that was a feature that the client was concerned about the most and wanted to test for. For this prototype, I used Invision.

View Invision prototype

USABILITY TESTING

10 testers who fit within the persona molds were chosen for the usability test. This was a good opportunity for me to get feedback on the 3D rooms shopping experience as well as to catch anything I was missing. Some of the feedback that I got was:

  • Testers valued the 3D rooms because they could see the products and get a sense of scale and how it could look in their homes.
  • All testers understood what products were clickable because of the orange markings.
  • Testers wanted to know technical information and what would come with the purchase.

THE STUMBLES

A problem that I faced in this project was that user research was not properly conducted. The information that I gathered for my user personas was based off of what the client provided, as well as research that I did online. Other stumbles included keeping track when designing screens since I was working on this alone.
